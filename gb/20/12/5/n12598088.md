  <p><ahref="/gb/tag/%E9%9D%92%E6%98%A5.md#1">青春</a>可以比作夏天吧<br />  可她的美更加温柔&emsp;动人&emsp;看啊<br />  粗野的风&emsp;总想吹落五月迷人的花蕾（*）<br />  夏天的好日子太短了<br />  偶尔&emsp;太阳当空时太热了<br />  时常&emsp;乌云遮盖了金色的阳光<br />  一切美好的东西都会衰退<br />  是随机的&emsp;还是大自然的魔力<br />  然而&emsp;不朽的是青春呵<br />  她拥有的美丽不会消失<br />  死亡也奈何不了她<br />  这永恒的诗行&emsp;让青春随着时间成长<br />  只要人能够呼吸&emsp;眼睛能够看见<br />  只要生命不息&emsp;青春就有活力</p>
  <p>注：<br />  标题“永恒的青春”为译者所加。</p>
  <p>(*)在<ahref="/gb/tag/%E8%8E%8E%E5%A3%AB%E6%AF%94%E4%BA%9A.md#1">莎士比亚</a>时代, 五月已是夏季。这个网页也出了解释：<br />  http://www.shakespeares-sonnets.com/xviiicomm.md#1</p>
  <p>莎士比亚的原英文诗<br />  William Shakespeare, Sonnet XVIII</p>
  <p>Shall I compare thee to a summer&#8217;s day?<br />  Thou art more lovely and more temperate:<br />  Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,<br />  And summer&#8217;s lease hath all too short a date:<br />  Sometime too hot the eye of heaven shines,<br />  And often is his gold complexion dimmed,<br />  And every fair from fair sometime declines,<br />  By chance, or nature&#8217;s changing course untrimmed:<br />  But thy eternal summer shall not fade,<br />  Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow&#8217;st,<br />  Nor shall death brag thou wander&#8217;st in his shade,<br />  When in eternal lines to time thou grow&#8217;st,<br />  So long as men can breathe, or eyes can see,<br />  So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.@</p>
