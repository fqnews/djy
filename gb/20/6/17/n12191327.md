<p>【单字/词解析】</p>
<p>● crime，名词/kra?m/犯罪<br />
自然发音：crime<br />
英解：illegal activities<br />
例句：It is an undeniable fact that drug-related crime is increasing.（不可否认！与毒品有关的犯罪正在增加。）<br />
用法：从事犯罪行为（commit crimes）、被（法律）指控有罪（be accused of a crime，例如：Jacky is accused of a murder.（Jacky被指控谋杀。）</p>
<p>● defund，动词/?di??f?nd/停止为&#8230;提供资金（削减预算）<br />
自然发音：de-FUND<br />
英解：to stop providing the money to pay for something<br />
例句：Trump opposes any proposal to defund US police.（川普反对任何要削减美国警察预算的<br />
建议。）<br />
用法：字首“de-”指“减少、删除”，例如：delete（删除）、decrease（减少）、deduct（减去）等。</p>
<p>● abandon，动词/??b?n.d?n/舍弃<br />
自然发音：a-BAN-don<br />
英解：to leave a place, thing, or person, usually for ever<br />
例句：We decided to abandon the first draft of the report and start over.（我们决定放弃报告的第一版初稿，从新再做第二版本。）<br />
用法：沉溺于&#8230;（abandon yourself to sth）。例如：Don&#8217;t abandon yourself to smart phone games!（不要沉溺于手游！）</p>
<p>● rate，名词/re?t/率<br />
自然发音：rate<br />
英解：the amount or number of times it happens<br />
例句：The survey found no correspondence between crime and unemployment rates.（该问卷调查指出，犯罪率和失业率之间没有对应关系。）<br />
用法：当动词指“评估”，例如：How would you rate his work on a scale of 1 to 5?（你觉得他的工作能力如何？1-5分，5分最高。）</p>
<p>● constantly，副词/?k?nst?ntli/不断地<br />
自然发音：CONS-tant-ly<br />
英解：always or regularly<br />
例句：Andrew has his TV on constantly before sleeping.（安德鲁在睡觉前，经常要将电视开着。）<br />
用法：constancy，名词/?k?nst?nsi/恒久不变，例如：We live in a world without constancy.（我们活在一个变化多端的世界。）</p>
<p>● harass，动词/h??r?s/骚扰<br />
自然发音：ha-RASS<br />
英解：to continue to annoy or upset someone over a period of time<br />
例句：He uses his position of power to sexually harass his female employees.（他利用职权对女雇员进行性骚扰。）<br />
用法：harassed，形容词，“疲惫焦虑的”，例如：Dealing with too much trouble at a time, Yoki looks harassed.（一次处理太多麻烦事儿，Yoki看起来很焦躁疲惫。）</p>
<p><a name="_Toc2020061701"></a><br />
● illegal，形容词/??li?ɡ(?)l/非法的<br />
自然发音：i-LLE-gal<br />
英解：not allowed by law<br />
例句：It&#8217;s illegal to drive through a red light.（闯红灯是违法的。）<br />
用法：相似拼音字：illegible，形容词/??led??b?l/，（字迹）难以辨识，例如：His writing is almost illegible.（他的字迹（太潦草或写太轻）几乎难以辨认。）</p>
<p>● investigation，名词/?n?vest??ɡe???n/调查<br />
自然发音：in-ves-ti-GA-tion<br />
英解：the act or process of examining a crime, problem, statement, etc., especially to discover the truth<br />
例句：The government has promised a full investigation into the disaster.（政府已承诺对这场灾难进行全面调查。）<br />
用法：invest，动词，“投资、投入”，例如：She decided to invest in her future by taking a management course.（她决定参加管理课程来投资自己的未来。）</p>
<p>● hoax，名词/ho?ks/恶作剧、骗局<br />
自然发音：hoax<br />
英解：a plan to deceive someone or trick in which someone deliberately or tells people that something bad is going to happen<br />
例句：The bomb threat turned out to be a hoax.（炸弹威胁原来是个恶作剧。）<br />
用法：可以当动词，例如：Don&#8217;t hoax the new boy who&#8217;s my nephew.（不要对那个新来的男生恶作剧，他是我侄子。）</p>
<p>● Sleepy Joe 爱困乔/嗑睡乔<br />

<p><a name="_Toc2020061702"></a><br />
● unfair，形容词/?n?fer/不公平的<br />
自然发音：un-FAIR<br />
英解：not treating people in an equal way, or not morally right<br />
例句：Life can be unfair sometimes, but that&#8217;s no reason to give up on it.（人生有时是不公平的，但绝没有理由因此放弃。）<br />
用法：fair，形容词，“公平的、合理的”，例如：All the workers want is a fair wage for the work that they do.（工人要的，只是与他们的工作相称的合理薪资。）</p>
