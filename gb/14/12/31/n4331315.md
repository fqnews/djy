  <p>小时候，教科书上说东南亚某某国盛产柚木，可做家俱、船只什么的。心想：他们那地方有那么多<ahref="/gb/tag/%E6%9F%9A%E5%AD%90.md#1">柚子</a>吗？大家天天吃柚子也够幸福的啦。继之一想：“柚木”不会是“柚子树”吧？据我小时候看到的柚子树，说要拿来做家俱，那材质是差太远了。</p>
  <p>这张图，我们把柚子“主体”摆在中左方；右边再安置一个“宾体”，以取得画面的平衡。在中间“桥段”部位，再飞来两只小鹦鹉，做为过渡，就像音乐里的“过门”。</p>
  <p></p>
  <h2>Pomelo / ink and color painting 柚子（<ahref="/gb/tag/%E5%BD%A9%E5%A2%A8.md#1">彩墨</a>） </h2>  <p>There are many people who have eaten pomelos all their lives, but have never seen a pomelo tree. As for me, I&#8217;ve seen pomelo trees all my life, but had never painted a pomelo. That&#8217;s how the notion struck me to paint a pomelo tree. </p>
  <p>During childhood, our textbooks stated that such and such countries in Southeast Asia produced teak, which is used to make furniture and boats, etc. Since the Chinese characters for &#8220;teak&#8221;—&#8221;youmu&#8221;—make it sound like teak is the wood of the pomelo tree, I wondered if there were really so many pomelo trees in those places. If so, everyone would be fortunate enough to eat pomelos practically every day. I continued to wonder if teak wasn&#8217;t just the wood of the pomelo tree.But going by the pomelo trees that I had known during childhood, the wood seemed way too inferior to be used to make furniture. </p>
  <p>This painting places the pomelo tree &#8220;subject&#8221; in the center-left of the picture. The &#8220;object&#8221; has been placed on the right side, which gives the painting a sense of balance. The central &#8220;bridging section&#8221; features two small flying parrots, which serve the same function as a transition in music. @</p>
