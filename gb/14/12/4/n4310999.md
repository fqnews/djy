  <p>凌霄，多好听的名字，壮志冲上云霄。说明凌霄的特性：一心往上爬。不过，后来听说凌霄必须依附在墙垣或大树的枝干才能往上爬，没了依附，它就倒了下来。真不知倒地匍匐的凌霄会是什么景况啊。</p>
  <p>人类社会不就是这个样子吗？自己不努力，依附裙带或特殊什么关系坐到什么大位，一旦依附的权贵倒下，树倒猢狲散，变得景况凄凉，不也是很可悲吗？</p>
  <p>Trumpet Creeper / ink and color painting 凌霄（<ahref="/gb/tag/%E5%BD%A9%E5%A2%A8.md#1">彩墨</a>）</p>
  <p>I once went to the botanical garden in Taipei to sketch around 10 years ago, and I happened to catch the trumpet creeper in bloom. The red blossoms of the creeper were intoxicating, and sights in the garden were incomparably beautiful. </p>
  <p>The trumpet creeper&#8217;s Chinese name means &#8220;soaring into the sky,&#8221; which alludes to this plant&#8217;s fondness for climbing. However, the creeper must eventually depend on a wall or the branches of a tree if it is to keep climbing. Without anything to support it, it would fall over. I really can&#8217;t imagine what a trumpet creeper crawling on the ground would look like. </p>
  <p>Isn’t human society a bit like this? We often obtain our positions not through hard work, but through our in-laws or other special relationships. As soon as the powerful people we have attached ourselves to topple, we have to scramble to survive under humble circumstances—isn’t this pathetic? ＠</p>
