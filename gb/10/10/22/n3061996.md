<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>新泽西今年11月2日选举概况</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2020/03/GettyImages-1196292480-2-600x400-1-320x200.jpg" />
<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【大纪元10月22日讯】(大纪元记者世桑新泽西报导) 全美公民登记选民将于11月2日投票日根据各自居住地区状况投票。今年是<ahref="/gb/tag/%E4%B8%AD%E6%9C%9F%E9%80%89%E4%B8%BE.md#1">中期选举</a>年(也是选民对总统任期处理经济疲弱和高失业率是否满意的一次公投)。所有选区要选联邦众议员；一些选区要选联邦参议员和州长；一些选区要选州议员和地方官员；很多社区将选举市长、市郡官员、法官、警长等地方官员。目前36州的选民正在对州府税和地方税之升降以及债券发行，州府预算和政府管理等具体地方事务政策表达意见。</p>
<p>政党对州长和州议会的控制权，对2010年选区即将重划具有全国性的重要意义。拥有多数席位党有权制订未来10年选举中有利于其候选人的区界。今年共和党呈现强势，这与2006 年和2008 年相反，类似的转变也出现在选民的党派结构上，这是政治气候之下政党忠诚度随时间改变的反应。</p>
<p>新州府选举机构 (The State Elections Division) 的数据表明，新州今年登记选民有527万，比2009年的522万多。民主党今年为175万，2009年为177万；共和党比2009年的107万多了1万。近50%的登记选民244万不属于任何党派，比2009年增加了4万。</p>
<p>新泽西选民将在13个选区投票联邦众议员侯选人。与华裔居民利益相关联的侯选人 (依据字母排列) 主要在以下几个选区：<br />6区：<br />Jack Freudenheim (Independent)<br />Anna C. Little (Republican)<br />Frank Pallone, Jr. (Democratic)<br />Karen Ann Zaletel (Green Tea Patriots)<br />7区：<br />Leonard Lance (Republican)<br />Ed Potosnak (Democratic)<br />8区：<br />Raymond Giangrasso (Independent)<br />Bill Pascrell, Jr. (Democratic)<br />Roland Straten (Republican)<br />9区：<br />Michael A. Agosta (Republican)<br />Patricia Alessandrini (Green Party)<br />Steven R. Rothman (Democratic)<br />11区：<br />Rodney P. Frelinghusysen (Republican)<br />Jim Gawron (Libertarian Party)<br />Douglas Herbert (Democratic)<br />The League of Women Voters of New Jersey<br />12区：<br />Kenneth J. Cody (Truth Vision Hope)<br />Rush Holt (Democratic)<br />Scott Siprelle (Republican)</p>
<p>全美选举事务方面的草根组织“妇女选举联盟(LWV.org)”之新泽西分部，为侯选人拟出相关的10个问题，选民可以通过他们各种各样竞选活动(演讲，辩论，广告等)体现的回答内容，来作为自己判断和决定选举人的参考资料。<br />1. What should be the federal government&#8217;s role in funding and regulating K-12 public education?<br />2. Do you agree or disagree with the US Supreme Court&#8217;s decision in Citizens United V. Federal Election Commission? Should Congress pass new legislation as a response to the Court&#8217;s decision? Please explain your answer.<br />3. What economic policies should the federal government pursue during this recession?<br />4. What should the US short-term and long-term goals be in Iraq and Afghanistan and how should these be achieved?<br />5. Do you support or oppose the health care financing reforms enacted in 2010? What aspects (if any) would you change?<br />6. Many students are struggling with the rising costs of tuition for higher education. What actions, if any, should the federal government take to address this situation?<br />7. Gender inequities and biases persist in our society, whether in professional opportunities (women are paid about 77% of what men are paid for the same work) or healthcare coverage. Is it Congress&#8217; role to reduce these inequities and biases, and if so, what should be implemented to do so?<br />8. Would you vote to adopt a cap and trade system or a carbon tax? Please explain your answer.<br />9. The nation&#8217;s infrastructure (including highways, bridges, railroads, and tunnels) is aging. How would you approach this problem?<br />10. Do you support a path to legalization for those presently in the country without authorization? If not, what actions on the part of federal or state government do you believe would improve the current situation? If yes, please specify the steps that path should involve.</p>
