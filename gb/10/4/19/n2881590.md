<p>Obama Meets Dalai Lama Despite Chinese Warning<br />尽管中国警告，奥巴马会见达赖喇嘛<br /> <embed src="http://www.youmaker.com/v.swf" width="450" b="358" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" allowFullScreen="true"flashvars="file=http://www.youmaker.com/video/v%3Fid%3D75600c77e6fd4e7ea0e479318b045f3f001%26nu%3Dnu&#038;showdigits=true&#038;overstretch=fit&#038;autostart=false&#038;rotatetime=12&#038;linkfromdisplay=false&#038;repeat=list&#038;shuffle=false&#038;&#038;showfsbutton=false&#038;fsreturnpage=&#038;fullscreenpage=" /> <br />本新闻影音出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻<br />http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_na/2010-02-19/563647982014.md#1</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B>brush aside</B> : ph.漠视、不理会<br />2. <B>commend</B>: vt. 称赞<br />3. <B>commitment</B>:n.致力、献身<br />4.<B> pursuit</B>: n. 追求<br />5.<B> envoy</B>: n. 使者;外交使节<br />6. <B>yield</B>: vt. 产生<br />7. <B>defy</B>: vt. 向&#8230;挑战;激,惹<br />8.<B> pains</B>: n. 千辛万苦,呕心沥血<br />9. <B>coverage</B>: n. 新闻报导<br />10.<B> placate</B>: vt. 抚慰;和解<br />11.<B> regard as</B>: ph.把&#8230;视为<br />12. <B>separatist</B>: n.分离主义者</p>
<p>U.S. President Barack Obama hosted the Dalai Lama at the WhiteHouse yesterday,<B> brushing aside</B>China&#8217;s warning that the talks with the spiritual leader could further damage strained Sino-U.S. ties. <br />美国总统奥巴马昨天在白宫以主人身份招待达赖喇嘛，不理会中国的警告：与精神领袖对话，可能进一步伤害中美关系。</p>
<p>The meeting took place in the face of wider tensions over U.S. weapons sales to Taiwan, Beijing&#8217;s currency policies and Chinese Internet security. <br />会面发生在面对广泛的紧张情势：美国对台军售、北京货币政策及中国互联网的防护。</p>
<p>After the hour-long meeting, the White House said in a written statement &#8220;The president <B>commended</B> the Dalai Lama&#8217;s &#8230;<B> commitment </B>to nonviolence and his<B> pursuit </B>of dialogue with the Chinese government.&#8221;<br />在近一小时的会面之后，白宫发表声明表示：总统赞扬达赖喇嘛致力于非暴力及寻求与中国政府的对话。”</p>
<p>Obama encouraged China and the Dalai Lama&#8217;s<B> envoys</B> to keep up efforts to resolve their differences through negotiations, despite recent talks having yielded</B> little progress. <br />奥巴马鼓励中国及达赖喇嘛的特使，继续透过协商努力解决他们的差异，尽管最近的会谈已经有了一些些的进展。</p>
<p>The White House said Obama and the Dalai Lama also &#8220;agreed on the importance of a positive and cooperative relationship between the United States and China.&#8221; <br />白宫说，奥巴马及达赖喇嘛也“同意美国与中国之间积极的合作关系是很重要的。”</p>
<p>While <B>defying </B>Chinese demands to scrap the talks, the White House took<B> pains </B>to keep the encounter low-key, barring media <B>coverage</B> of the meeting itself, in an apparent bid to <B>placate </B>Beijing. <br />无视于中国取消对话的要求，白宫费尽心机使会面能够低调，禁止媒体采访本次会议，显然是为了安抚北京。</p>
<p>Beijing has faced international criticism for its Tibet policies. It is being encouraged to preserve Tibet&#8217;s identity and protect its people&#8217;s human rights. <br />北京的西藏政策一直面对国际的批评。国际上鼓励维护西藏的认同，及保护人民的权利。</p>
<p>The Dalai Lama is admired by millions around the world as a man of peace, but Beijing<B> regards</B> him as a dangerous separatist.<br />全世界数百万人夸赞达赖喇嘛为和平人士，但是北京把他看作是危险的分离主义者。</p>
