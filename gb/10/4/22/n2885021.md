  <p>本新闻影音出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻<br />http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_na/2010-02-10/087209977669.md#1<br /><b>【新闻关键字】</b><br />1. <b>strut</b> ：v. 很有自信的呈现，炫耀<br />2. <b>break out </b>：phr. 摆脱<br />3. <b>exotic</b> ：adj. 异国风情的<br />4.<b> sophisticated</b>：adj. 优雅的<br />5. <b>lush</b>：adj. 华丽的<br />6.<b> luxurious</b>：adj. 精美昂贵的<br />7. <b>mill</b>：n. 纺织厂<br />8.<b> tweed</b>：n. 花呢</p>
  <p>Models and designers are preparing to <b>strut </b>their stuff at New York Fashion Week, which will showcase the Fall Winter 2010 collections.<br />模特儿和设计师们正准备着在纽约时装周呈现他们的作品，也就是2010年秋冬装系列。 </p>
  <p>As a weak economy continues to cast a shadow over the $45 billion dollar fashion retail market, some designers feel it&#8217;s time to <b>break out</b>.<br />由于经济的疲软持续笼罩着超过450亿美元的时装零售市场，有些设计师觉得是摆脱这阴霾的时候了。 </p>
  <p>Designer Devi Kroell is known for her <b>exotic</b> and<b> sophisticated</b> styles, and for using<b> lush </b>fabrics for her collection.<br />设计师Devi Kroell她的设计风格优雅且充满异国风情而因此出名，此外她所设计的服装都是使用华丽的布材。 </p>
  <p>[Devi Kroell, Designer]:<br />&#8220;The materials are super <b>luxurious</b>. I mean, you have the beautiful silks that I developed with the <b>mills</b> in Italy, France, and beautiful <b>tweed </b>fabrics that I developed with English mills, so it&#8217;s really about stepping the luxury game up. This might sound a little bit strange in these economic times, but today, I think, if you really want to motivate people to buy something, it really has to be the best, and it has to be a really <b>emotional buy</b>, or they won&#8217;t buy.&#8221;<br />[Devi Kroell, 设计师]:<br />“这些布材都非常非常的精美昂贵的。我的意思是，你拥有这些美丽的丝绸都是我分别在意大利、法国的纺织厂研制而来的，而这美丽的花呢面料是我在英国的纺织厂所开发的，所以这一切的布料材质都使作品更加精美昂贵。在目前的经济情况下，这听起来有点奇怪，但今天，我认为，如果你真的想鼓励人们购买东西，它就真的必须是最好的，而且它必须让人真的想拥有，否则他们将不会购买。“</p>
  <p><b>【新闻关键字】</b><br />9.<b> pack up</b>：phr. 打包<br />10.<b> iconic</b>：adj. 有代表性的<br />11. <b>dire</b>：adj. 恐怖的、可怕的<br />12.<b> trend</b>: n. 趋势<br />13. <b>emerge</b>：v. 浮现、出现<br />14. <b>rhythm</b>: n. 旋律 </p>
  <p>After 15 years, New York Fashion week is <b>packing up</b> its tents at Bryant Park and moving to Lincoln Center.<br />15年后，纽约时装周从在Bryant公园搭建账棚，搬移到林肯中心。 </p>
  <p>[Fern Mallis, Director New York Fashion Week]:<br />&#8220;You know, that, like everything else in the world, things change and <b>iconic</b> buildings come down and stadiums, you know, get destroyed and re-built in new places and so, we are fashion&#8217;s &#8216;Yankee Stadium&#8217; this season.&#8221;<br />[Fern Mallis, 纽约时装周主任]:<br />“你知道的，就跟世界其他地方一样，周遭的事物会改变，而那些具有代表性建筑会逐渐没落，就像运动场，你知道的，会被摧毁然后在新的地方重新建造，所以我们就像是这一季时尚界的&#8217;洋基球场&#8217;。” <br />Mallis says that despite so much <b>dire </b>economic news, she does see some positive<b> trends emerging </b>in the world of fashion.<br />Mallis说，尽管有这么多负面的经济新闻，但她确实看到了一些正面的趋势，出现在时尚界里。 </p>
  <p>[Fern Mallis, Director New York Fashion Week]:</p>
  <p>&#8220;I don&#8217;t think it&#8217;s perfect but I think people have figured out a<b> rhythm</b> of how to do business in this kind of climate. I think it&#8217;s been an interesting learning curve for a lot of designers and a lot of companies to really focus strongly on who their customer is and not try to be all things to all people.&#8221;<br />[Fern Mallis, New York Fashion Week的主任]:<br />“我不认为这是完美的，但我认为，人们已经找到了如何在这种情况下做生意的节奏感。我认为这是一个有趣的学习曲线，让许多设计师和公司把重点强烈地放在谁才是自己的客户，而不试图将所有的事情推给所有的人。“ </p>
  <p>New York Fashion week is expected to bring over $200 million in visitor spending to the city.<br />此次纽约时装周，参访者在纽约的消费预计带来超过 2亿美元的收入。<br />@*<br /> <font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
