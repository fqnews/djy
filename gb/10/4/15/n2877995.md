  <p>Beijing&#8217;s &#8220;I&#8217;m looking for you&#8221; supermarket sells nothing but love.<br />The market which was opened in November 2009 gives the city&#8217;s singles a new way to meet &#8220;the one.&#8221;<br />北京的“我正在找你”超市，只卖爱情这个商品。<br />这家于2009年11月开幕的超市，给了城市中的单身者一个新方法，去遇见他们人生中的“那个人”。</p>
  <p>In their few months of operation, the market has more than 1,000 <b>clients </b>and successfully matched over fifty pairs. <br />在他们只有几个月的经营之后，这个市场已经有了超过1000个委托者，和不只50对的成功配对。</p>
  <p>25-year-old teacher Qu Hui: &#8220;I really hope I can find someone here; that is my greatest hope from the love supermarket. But if that doesn&#8217;t happen, I hope I can use this space to meet more friends and <b>acquaintances</b>. After all, I am a teacher, so the people that I can meet and <b>socialize</b> with are very limited.&#8221;<br />25岁的曲慧老师说：“我真的希望我可以在这找到另一半；那是我在爱情超市最大的希望。但是如果没有发生，我希望我可以运用这个空间去认识更多的朋友和熟人。毕竟，我是一个老师，在我周遭可遇到的和能够社交的，都非常的有限。”</p>
  <p>Each customer pays three dollars, puts up his or her<b> profile</b>, which includes name, age, occupation, income and a picture they hope will catch the eye of their perfect mate. Then, browse around.<br />每个客人支付三元，放上他或她的简介，其中包含姓名、年纪、职业、收入，和一张他们认为最能吸引完美伴侣的照片。然后，四处浏览。</p>
  <p>35-year-old Wang Jiaohong said the supermarket has a lot of great women.<br />35岁的王兆鸿说，这家超市有很多很棒的女生。</p>
  <p>[Wang Jiaohong, Searching for Love]:<br />&#8220;My mother says that the reason why I haven&#8217;t found the one yet is because I&#8217;m too picky. But in reality, I believe it&#8217;s because I just haven&#8217;t yet found my match.&#8221;<br />[寻找爱情的王兆鸿]:<br />&#8220;我母亲说，我没有找到我的爱情的原因，是因为我太挑了。但实际上，我相信这是因为我还没有找到和我相配的人。</p>
  <p><b>Eager </b>mothers have encouraged their single children to attend the market, <b>praising </b>it as a safe space because each participant&#8217;s information is <b>validated</b>.<br />渴望的妈妈们鼓励他们的单身小孩去参加这家超市，称许那是一个安全的地方，因为每一位参加者的资讯都是有效的。</p>
