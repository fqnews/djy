<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻学英语：纽约餐车摊贩也用推特</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2020/04/1024px-China_Han_Dynasty_1-320x200.jpg" />
<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>New York Food Vendors Learn to Twitter</B><br />纽约餐车摊贩学会用推特<br /><embed src="http://www.youmaker.com/v.swf" width="450" b="358" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" allowFullScreen="true"flashvars="file=http://www.youmaker.com/video/v%3Fid%3Dcc464be272114c258a30ceedf77e9046001%26nu%3Dnu&#038;showdigits=true&#038;overstretch=fit&#038;autostart=false&#038;rotatetime=12&#038;linkfromdisplay=false&#038;repeat=list&#038;shuffle=false&#038;&#038;showfsbutton=false&#038;fsreturnpage=&#038;fullscreenpage=" /> <br />本新闻影音出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻：<br />http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_na/2010-03-20/414980947332.md#1<br /><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B> vendor</B>:n. 摊贩<br />2.<B>twitter</B>: n. 推特 (微网志; 一种免费的网路社群工具)<br />3.<B>open for business</B>: ph. 开张<br />4.<B>licensed</B>: adj. 得到正式许可的<br />5.<B>tend to</B>: ph. 倾向<br />6. <B> adjustment </B>:n. 调整<br />7.<B>immediacy</B>: n. 即时性</p>
<p>STORY:<br />As soon as Kim Ima, finds a parking spot for her Treats Truck in New York City, she tweets her approximately 3,000 Twitter followers to let them know she&#8217;s <B>open for business</B>. <br />金伊玛在纽约市一找到了停放它黄柏餐车的地方，便开始利用推特，让约3,000名推特订户知道她开始营业了。</p>
<p>[Kim Ima, Treats Truck Owner]:<br />&#8220;Twitter is amazing because you can tell people right away, &#8216;Got here 15 minutes early,&#8217; then they know, hey the Treats Truck is there, or, you can communicate with people and you know that they&#8217;ve signed up to follow you.&#8221;<br />[金伊玛，黄柏餐车主]：<br />“推特真是棒极了，因为你可以立刻用它告诉大家，你提早十五分钟到了，所以大家就会知道黄柏餐车已经来了。或者，你也可以用它和顾客沟通，然后你会知道他们订阅了你的推特。”</p>
<p>There are about 3,000 <B>licensed</B> food vending trucks in New York City, according to the Street Vendor Project.<br />根据街头摊贩计划的报告，纽约市大约有3000辆有执照的餐车摊贩。</p>
<p>Most food trucks <B>tend to </B>stay at one location, but Ima&#8217;s Treats Truck is in a different spot every day.<br />大部分的餐车都倾向待在相同的一个地点，但是伊玛的黄柏餐车每天都在不同地点出现。</p>
<p>One of the advantages of using Twitter is that Ima can let customers know about any last-minute<B> adjustments</B>. <br />使用推特的其中一个好处，是让伊玛可以在任何时刻，让她的顾客知道她最新调整的状况。</p>
<p> [Kim Ima, Treats Truck Owner]: <br />&#8220;It&#8217;s New York City, so, so, it&#8217;s always an adventure so it&#8217;s good to have a way to communicate with people.&#8221;<br />[金伊玛，黄柏餐车主]：<br />“这里是纽约市，在这里就像是在探险一样。所以，有个方法可以和人们沟通，是很棒的一件事。”</p>
<p>Customers also appreciate the <B>immediacy</B> of the twittering vendors. <br />顾客对于摊贩使用推特带来的即时性，也感到很高兴。</p>
<p>[Jason Haft, Food Truck Customer and Twitter User]:<br />&#8220;It&#8217;s good, so, I want to know where they&#8217;re at and if it&#8217;s going to be a long time.&#8221;<br />[杰森海夫特，餐车顾客以及推特使用者]：<br />“这样很棒，它可以让我知道餐车现在在哪，以及估算我走过去要花多少时间。”</p>
<p><p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B> rickshaw</B>: n. 人力车; 黄包车<br />2.<B>dumpling </B>:n. 饺子<br />3. <B> keep in touch with</B>: ph. 与…保持联系<br />4. <B> super-duper </B>:adj. 超级; 极度<br />5.<B>estimate</B>: v. 评价</p>
<p>[EJ Cory, Food Truck Customer and Twitter User]:<br />&#8220;Especially for someone like Kim with St. Patrick&#8217;s day Parade on Wednesday, she wasn&#8217;t sure if she was going to park here. So with Twitter she was able to provide location updates and we knew where to find her.&#8221;<br />[EJ柯瑞，餐车顾客以及推特使用者]：<br />“特别是像金伊玛这样的，如果遇到礼拜三圣派翠克游行，她不晓得她是否能够在这里设摊。所以，使用推特她就可以向大家更新她的位置讯息，我们也可以知道要去哪里才找的到她。”</p>
<p>[Madeline Caulfield, Food Truck Customer and Twitter User]: <br />&#8220;It&#8217;s instant you know, they can tweet exactly where they are exactly when they&#8217;re there and then I can go on my phone or whatever and I can check and make sure that they&#8217;re where I expect them to be.&#8221; <br />[玛德琳，餐车顾客以及推特使用者]：<br />“你知道的，它很即时，当他们到了那里，他们可以很精确地推特他们的具体位置，然后我可以继续讲我的电话或做其它事情，然后再回头来确认他们是不是在我预期中他们会出现的地方。”</p>
<p>Kenny Lao parks his <B>Rickshaw Dumpling</B> truck next to the Treats Truck most Friday&#8217;s. He has been tweeting since his truck first hit the streets for business and says Twitter is an ideal tool to<B> keep in touch with</B> his 5,000 followers. <br />廖肯尼在大部分礼拜五时，会把它的人力饺子餐车停在黄柏餐车旁边。他从上街推餐车做生意开始，就开始用推特了。推特是一个让他和他的五千名推特订阅者保持联络的理想工具。</p>
<p>[Kenny Lao, Co-owner, Rickshaw Dumpling Truck]:<br />&#8220;People really depend on us to be at certain locations on every day of the week and they get <B>super-duper </B>excited for dumpling day and this is the best way for us to tell them, &#8216;Hey, we&#8217;re caught in traffic, we&#8217;re on our way up,&#8217; or &#8216;Hey we&#8217;re parked, get ready,&#8217; or &#8216;Here&#8217;s the special for the day.'&#8221; <br />[廖肯尼，合伙人，人力饺子餐车]：<br />“人们真的很需要我们每一周的每一天，出现在特定的位置。而且他们每到我卖饺子的日子就会变的超级兴奋。所以推特成为了我们与他们对话的最佳管道：‘嘿，我们在路上了，但被交通卡住了’，‘我们停好车了，准备开始卖喽！’，或是‘这是今天的特别餐点！’。”</p>
<p>It is<B> estimated</B> that food vendors have been a feature of New York City&#8217;s streets since the end of the 17th century.<br />据推估，自17世纪末以来，小吃摊就一直是纽约市街头的一项特色。<br />@*<br /> <font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
