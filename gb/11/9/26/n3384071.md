<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>许其正：红尾伯劳的哀诉</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2020/06/1812261554142483-320x200.jpg" />
<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>嘎嘎，我陷在人类鸟仔踏的陷阱里了（注）<br />我振翅欲飞，却为绳索所缚，吊在半空中<br />我呼天抢地，喊破喉咙，也是徒然<br />当飞得倦了，捕捉害虫累了<br />我站在孤枝顶上休息到底犯了什么罪？<br />嘎嘎，人类的良心何在？<br />嘎嘎，人类的良心何时才唤得醒？<br />人类为什么要贪婪得设下鸟子踏来陷害我？<br />人类为什么要忘恩负义，饕餮我的肉？<br />空有铁索般的脚爪又有何用？<br />空有锐利如刀的尖喙又于事何补？<br />我只能在这里嘎嘎地哀诉<br />我只能在这里挣扎、哭喊到伤心泪尽</p>
<p>虽然外表上和我的身体相比<br />我的翅膀显得娇小柔弱<br />常予人“载不动许多愁”之感<br />我私底下却为拥有它们而自感骄傲<br />它们具有惊人的大力量<br />强过海上的船只，空中的飞机<br />随着季节的变化，它们<br />强力地载着我，飞出诗经，南来北往<br />越过千里平畴大洋，万重高山峻岭<br />征服阴晦风雨，如火烈日<br />活得自由无羁，悠然自得</p>
<p>当然，不只是翅膀，我知道<br />我陀螺似的个子，不华丽的羽毛<br />我粗嘎的嗓音，锐利如刀的尖喙<br />我铁索般的脚爪，明亮如灯的眼睛<br />也是不起眼，不邀人宠爱的<br />不过，它们是我的一部分<br />和我的血肉相连，如胶似漆<br />我不能一刻没有它们<br />它们完全接受我的善意指挥<br />全心全力飞翔，跳跃，捕捉害虫<br />使我成为一只有益于世的漂鸟<br />我私底下仍为拥有它们而自感骄傲<br />不幸的是，嘎嘎，现在<br />我陷在人类鸟子踏的陷阱里了<br />不知道飞得倦了，捕捉害虫累了<br />我站在孤枝上休息到底犯了什么罪？<br />人类为什么要贪婪地设下鸟子踏来陷害我？<br />人类为什么要忘恩负义，饕餮我的肉，<br />我振翅欲飞，却为绳索所缚，吊在半空中<br />我呼天抢地，喊破喉咙，也是徒然<br />我铁索般的脚爪挣不脱陷阱<br />我锐利如刀的尖喙啄不断捆绑的绳索<br />我只能在这里嘎嘎地哀诉<br />人类呀，嘎嘎，你们的良心何在？<br />嘎嘎，如何才能唤醒你们的良心？</p>
<p>注：此处的鸟子踏是捕鸟人制作以捕捉红尾伯劳的专门装置。</p>
<p><b>A Red-tailed Shrike’s Sorrowful Pouring<br />　　　　　　　　　　　　Hsu ChiCheng</b></p>
<p>Ah, ah, I have fallen into the snare laid by human being*<br />I try my best to fly, but I’m tied in the air<br />I cry my eyes out, and it is fruitless<br />What law do I offend against when I work hard at<br />Catching harmful insects on the tree branches?<br />Ah, ah, where can one find mankind conscience?<br />Ah, ah, when will mankind conscience be aroused?<br />Why is human being so greedy that they lay the snare for me?<br />Why is human being so ungrateful that they kill me and swallow my body?<br />My claws look like iron chain, but they are no use<br />My beak is sharp and hard, but it’s helpless<br />I can only unbosom myself in grief here<br />I can only struggle, cry and weep in sorrow</p>
<p>My wings are so weaker<br />Than my body<br />Leaving others a sense of “unable to load so much sorrow”<br />Yet I feel proud to have them<br />They have an astonishing strength<br />Stronger than ships in the sea and planes in the air<br />With the seasonal variation, they carry me<br />Flying out of Classic of poetry<br />Flying over a thousand mountains and ten thousand rivers<br />Conquering the wind, the rain and the burning sun<br />Leading a free and easy life</p>
<p>Of course I know –not only my wings<br />But my round body, my rough feathers<br />My husky voice, my sharp and hard beak<br />My cable-like claws and even my bright eyes<br />Are looked down by human being<br />But each of them is a part of mine<br />They are all my own flesh and blood<br />I can not leave them even a while<br />They are all under my kind command<br />Wholeheartdly fly, jump and catch harmful insects<br />And let me become a beneficial bird<br />I feel proud to have them<br />Unfortunately, I have fallen<br />Into snare laid by human being<br />I don’t know what law I offend against<br />When I work hard at catching harmful insects on tree branches?<br />Why is human being so greedy that they lay the snare for me?<br />Why is human being so ungrateful that they kill me and swallow my body?<br />I try my best to fly, but I’m tied in the air<br />I cry my eyes out, and it’s fruitless<br />My cable-like claws can’t throw off the snare<br />My sharp beak can’t peck the rope broken<br />I can only unbosom myself in grief here<br />O, mankind, where can one find your conscience?<br />O, how can your conscience be aroused?<br />*Here the “snare”is an arrangement called “Niaozaita”,which is specially used to red-tailed shrikes.＠</p>
