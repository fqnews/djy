  <p>（接<ahref="/gb/16/8/17/n8211811.md#1" target="_blank">前文</a>）</p>
  <p><strong>6. 炸丸子阿嘎拉咭（Acarajé，又称炒酱饼干）</strong></p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212619" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171821072669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212619" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171821072669-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="333" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">刚炸好的阿嘎拉咭。(Claudia Baiana, Wikimedia Commons)</figcaption></figure>  <p>巴西最特别的街食当属阿嗄拉咭，这种小圆面包的做法是用黑眼豌豆泡水后打成糊，拌入洋葱泥，再用棕榈油炸透。将其破开，塞入满满的炖秋葵（caruru）或vatapa炒辣酱（椰奶、腰果、花生、虾米、面包粉等制成），再撒上干虾，现炸现吃，热辣鲜香。</p>
  <p>阿嗄拉咭据说起源于非洲，由黑奴带到了巴西东北部的巴伊亚（Bahia），成为当地的传统美食。</p>
  <p><strong>7. 椰蓉糕（Quindim）</strong></p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212759" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171822112669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212759" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171822112669-600x450.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="375" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">椰蓉糕。(Lúcia Bischoff, Wikimedia Commons)</figcaption></figure>  <p>黄色有光泽的椰蓉糕是巴伊亚的另一道美食，原料不超过四种：鸡蛋、糖和椰子（有的加黄油），在杯子蛋糕大小的模具中烤成，底部焦黄，密密的椰丝口感坚实；平整的上部则有如蛋挞般适口。</p>
  <p>这道甜点也是文化融合的典型产物，据说quindim一词来源于刚果语的kintiti（意为美味），做法则结合了葡式甜点对蛋黄的钟情。</p>
  <p><strong>8. 阿萨伊浆果（A?aí，又称巴西莓）</strong></p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212626" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171819292669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212626" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171819292669-600x800.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="667" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">超级食品阿萨伊浆果（巴西莓）是棕榈科植物。(Forest Starr and Kim Starr/Flickr)</figcaption></figure>  <figure id="attachment_8212623" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171821552669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212623" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171821552669-600x406.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="339" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">阿萨伊浆果（巴西莓）。(Camila Neves Rodrigues da Silva, Wikimedia Commons)</figcaption></figure>  <p>在数千种亚马逊水果中，作为“超级食品”的阿萨伊最为出名，这要归功于其抗氧化功效。传统上，土着部落会吃这种紫色硬浆果以增强体力，也会以果汁配鱼吃。上世纪80年代，它成为里约冲浪者的首选“能量零食”。全巴西的咖啡厅、面包房、酒吧和超市都能可找到阿萨伊沙冰或果泥（常配麦片和香蕉片）、果汁，甚至还有阿萨伊伏特加和阿萨伊啤酒出售。</p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212677" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171819252669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212677" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171819252669-600x450.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="375" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">阿萨伊浆果沙冰。(Cornelius Kibelka/Flickr)</figcaption></figure>  <p><strong>9. 豆子炖肉（Feijoada）</strong></p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212631" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171820042669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212631" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171820042669-600x385.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="321" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">豆子炖肉。(Wilfredorrh/Flickr)</figcaption></figure>  <p>豆子炖肉这道菜，是用泡黑豆、烟熏香肠和猪尾、猪脚、猪耳等肉块炖煮而成，从泡豆子到猪肉脱盐，老式做法需花一天一夜，所以巴西人大都是到餐馆和酒吧吃，并且只在周三和周六吃这道菜。</p>
  <p>炖肉上桌后，淋在香喷喷的米饭上，搭配切碎的油烫甘蓝、解腻的柳丁片、烤木薯粉和脆炸猪皮点心（pork scratchings），再喝些甘蔗酒助消化，最适合好友小聚享用。</p>
  <p>据说“黑豆杂煮”早先是给奴隶吃的膳食，如今加入高级食材，却变成最受欢迎的全民大菜。</p>
  <p><strong>10. 炸制酒吧小食（Fried bar snacks）</strong></p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212636" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608172336372669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212636" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608172336372669-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="333" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">脆炸木薯球（bolinho）。(Romerito Pontes/Flickr)</figcaption></figure>  <p>在烈日炎炎的巴西，很多人喜欢在酒吧喝冰镇啤酒时佐以各色油炸小食，葡式的是填以融化的奶酪馅料、牛肉末或软软的棕榈心，还有填咸鳕鱼馅的脆炸木薯球（bolinhos），以及鸡丝土豆泥滚面包屑炸成金黄的“小鸡腿棒”（coxinha）。</p>
  <figure id="attachment_8212635" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608172334112669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212635" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608172334112669-600x400.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="333" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">“小鸡腿棒”（coxinha）。(Romerito Pontes/Flickr)</figcaption></figure>  <figure id="attachment_8212633" style="width: 500px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><ahref="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171819322669.jpg"><img class="wp-image-8212633 size-full" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2016/08/1608171819322669.jpg" alt="" width="500" b="500" /></a><figcaption class="wp-caption-text">“小鸡腿棒”coxinha。(Marina Torchiari/Flickr)</figcaption></figure>  <p>责任编辑：茉莉</p>
